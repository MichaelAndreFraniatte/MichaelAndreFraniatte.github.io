Deadzone and Sensitivity

Set deadzones from 0 to 99 for sticks and 0 for triggers. Set a linear curves for aiming in game options and remove deadzones by yourself for sticks and also enable a cubic curves while setting a sensitivity for half turning while jumping with a normal jump. The cubic curves you set on axis X and Y for aiming shall be the same and adapted to your enjoy. When you play top down shooters, you can use cursor and remove window title or topmost, for having a true pointer. Like for FPS games, for top down shooters, you can use desktop cursor and change sensitivity. Maybe it can ask to always set window position and size, because moving cursor and clicking on window can move it or resize it. When you remove deadzones too much, it move like crazy, and when you don't remove deadzones enough, the deadzone is bigger. Concerning remote play, you can have a very good control for aiming, when you set well your firewalls, your network, and switching to the console screen, instead of your PC screen. Don't choose cloud gaming, but local remote play instead, be near from your network and hardwares, and use the good one dedicated application, like official one. Setting network security, like a DNS server is also important, for fluidic control. Less useless network packets you have, but letting good packets, with a good speed rate of internet, make the control for aiming better. You don't see latency of control, as for the audio, like this. Actually, with a custom controller, you can enjoy playing on console a lot. Often, particularly for old games on consoles, you don't have same sensitivity while aiming than not aiming, and it's not competitive at all. There's often a kind of latency implemented in games, when you toggle aiming or turning to maximum. It's important to have good settings in game options for being competitive, and scripting your custom controller isn't enough sometimes and with certain game options.
